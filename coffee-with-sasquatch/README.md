# Coffee with Sasquatch

**Book 1: Field Notes from Ocean Shores**

A comedy-philosophy hybrid about a 200-year-old Sasquatch named Derek who comes to Ocean Shores, Washington, and interviews the locals about being human.

## About This Project

Derek S. Bigfoot saw a sign that said "Come As You Are" and took it literally. Now he's conducting anthropological research by interviewing residents of Ocean Shores about what it means to be human. Armed with a coffee cup from 1987 (that he never drinks) and genuine curiosity, Derek explores loneliness, connection, and the absurdity of existence‚Äîone interview at a time.

## Format

Each episode combines:
- **Interview transcripts** with locals
- **Field notes** (Derek's observations)
- **Personal journal entries** (Derek's reflections)
- **External perspectives** (texts, emails from interview subjects)
- **Animation notes** for visual adaptation
- **Audio notes** for podcast/audiobook format

## Tone & Style

- **Genre**: Absurdist comedy meets existential philosophy
- **Influences**: Between Two Ferns, The Anthropocene Reviewed, Bo Burnham
- **Target Audience**: Adults 25-45 who appreciate honest, awkward humor
- **Length**: 30 episodes planned for Book 1

## Episodes

### Season 1: Ocean Shores Residents

1. **The Barista's Tolerance** - Linda discovers it's never too late (when a Sasquatch asks the right questions)
2. **Ummmmm... What.** - Tom the Librarian interviews Derek (role reversal reveals loneliness is shareable)
3. _Coming Soon: The Surfer's Waves_ - Marcus searches for perfect waves and perfect meaning

## Characters

- **Derek S. Bigfoot**: 8'2", 201 years old, anthropologist, extremely literal, deeply curious
- **Linda Hargrove**: Barista/owner, 42, competent but stuck, reconsidering her narrative  
- **Tom Richardson**: Librarian, 38, anxious but kind, Derek's first friend
- **The Coffee Cup**: 1987 gas station cup, never consumed, eternal companion

## Themes

- Loneliness and connection
- Being vs. performing personhood
- Ocean Shores as refuge for misfits
- Comfort objects and their meaning
- Asking the right questions changes everything
- "Too late" is usually a story, not a fact
